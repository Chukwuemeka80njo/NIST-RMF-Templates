# PERSONNEL SECURITY POLICY

Version: 1.0  
Approval Date: (insert date)  
Owner: Human Resources (HR) & Information Security Officer (ISO)  
Classification: Internal / Restricted  

---

## **1. Purpose**
The purpose of this Personnel Security Policy is to ensure that individuals occupying positions of responsibility within the organization are trustworthy, meet security requirements, and understand their security responsibilities. This policy aligns with NIST SP 800-53 PS (Personnel Security) control family.

---

## **2. Scope**
This policy applies to:

- All employees, contractors, volunteers, and interns  
- All third-party personnel requiring access to organizational systems or facilities  
- All stages of employment (pre-employment, active employment, and termination)  

---

## **3. Policy Statements**

### **3.1 Position Risk Designation**
- All positions must be assigned a risk level (Low, Moderate, or High).  
- Position sensitivity must be documented and reviewed annually.  
- Higher-risk positions require additional background checks and oversight.

### **3.2 Background Screening**
- Background checks must be conducted **prior to onboarding**, including:  
  - Identity verification  
  - Criminal history  
  - Employment history  
  - Education verification  
  - Security clearance validation (if applicable)  
- Checks must meet federal, state, and regulatory requirements.  
- Employees must report any changes to their legal or criminal status.

### **3.3 Access Authorization**
- Access to systems and facilities must follow least privilege and need-to-know principles.  
- System access must not be granted until background checks are completed and approved.  
- Privileged access requires additional approvals and monitoring.

### **3.4 Personnel Termination**
- Access to all systems must be revoked **immediately** upon termination.  
- Terminated personnel must return all organizational equipment and badges.  
- HR must coordinate with IT to ensure timely offboarding.

### **3.5 Personnel Transfer**
- Transfers must trigger an access review.  
- Access inconsistent with the new role must be removed.  
- Training requirements must be updated accordingly.

### **3.6 Security Awareness Training**
- All users must complete security awareness training:  
  - Upon hire  
  - Annually  
  - As needed following incidents or major threats  
- Specialized role-based training must be provided for privileged users.

### **3.7 Duty Separation**
- No single individual should control critical functions end-to-end.  
- Segregation of duties must be implemented for sensitive processes.  

### **3.8 Sanctions for Non-Compliance**
- Violations of security policies may result in:  
  - Mandatory retraining  
  - Access reduction  
  - Disciplinary action  
  - Termination  

### **3.9 Third-Party Personnel**
- Contractors must follow the same personnel security requirements as employees.  
- Third-party access must be monitored and documented.  
- Vendor personnel must sign security and confidentiality agreements.

---

## **4. Roles & Responsibilities**

### **Human Resources (HR)**
- Oversees background checks  
- Maintains personnel files  
- Manages onboarding and termination workflows  

### **Information Security Officer (ISO)**
- Establishes personnel security requirements  
- Reviews personnel risk designations  
- Ensures compliance with NIST PS controls  

### **Managers & Supervisors**
- Ensure personnel meet requirements for their roles  
- Report performance or behavioral concerns  

### **Employees**
- Must comply with security training and reporting requirements  
- Must protect organizational information and assets  

---

## **5. Compliance**
Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action, access revocation, or legal penalties.  
Failure to perform required background checks or offboarding activities may result in audit findings and regulatory non-compliance.

---

## **6. Review Cycle**
This policy must be reviewed **annually** or upon major organizational changes.
