# CONTINGENCY PLANNING POLICY

Version: 1.0  
Approval Date: (insert date)  
Owner: Information Security Officer (ISO)  
Classification: Internal / Restricted  

---

## **1. Purpose**
The purpose of this Contingency Planning Policy is to ensure the organization can continue essential operations and recover critical systems following a disruption, disaster, cyberattack, or emergency event.

---

## **2. Scope**
This policy applies to:

- All organizational information systems  
- All business units responsible for continuity of operations  
- All employees, contractors, and third parties supporting critical services  

---

## **3. Policy Statements**

### **3.1 Business Impact Analysis (BIA)**
- A Business Impact Analysis must be conducted at least **annually**.  
- The BIA must identify critical systems, recovery priorities, and acceptable downtime thresholds (RTO/RPO).  

### **3.2 Contingency Plans**
The organization must maintain the following documented plans:

- **Continuity of Operations Plan (COOP)**  
- **Disaster Recovery Plan (DRP)**  
- **Incident Response Plan (IRP)**  
- **IT Service Continuity Plan**  
- **Crisis Communication Plan**

All plans must:

- Define roles and responsibilities  
- Include step-by-step recovery procedures  
- Identify backup resources and alternate sites  

### **3.3 Backup Requirements**
- Critical systems must be backed up according to documented schedules.  
- Backups must be **encrypted** and stored at an alternate secure location.  
- Backup restoration tests must be conducted **quarterly**.

### **3.4 Alternate Worksite**
- The organization must maintain an alternate work location or remote-work capability.  
- Staff must be able to operate critical functions from the alternate site.

### **3.5 Plan Testing**
- Contingency plans must be tested **at least annually** using one or more of the following:  
  - Tabletop exercises  
  - Functional tests  
  - Full-scale failover or disaster simulations  
- Test results must be documented, including corrective actions.

### **3.6 Plan Maintenance**
- Plans must be updated whenever:  
  - Systems change  
  - Personnel change  
  - Facilities or infrastructure change  
  - Regulatory requirements change  
- All plans must undergo a **formal annual review**.

### **3.7 Emergency Communications**
- Communication procedures must support operations during disruptions.  
- Emergency contact lists must be maintained and updated quarterly.  
- Incident information must be coordinated with senior leadership and relevant stakeholders.

---

## **4. Roles & Responsibilities**

### **Information Security Officer (ISO)**
- Oversees contingency planning efforts.  
- Approves and distributes contingency plans.  

### **Business Continuity Manager**
- Coordinates plan development, testing, and maintenance.  
- Ensures critical business functions are identified and prioritized.

### **System Owners**
- Ensure system-level recovery procedures exist and are regularly tested.  
- Maintain updated system documentation and contacts.

### **Employees**
- Must know their roles during emergencies and follow procedures outlined in contingency plans.

---

## **5. Compliance**
Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action or contractual penalties.  
Regulated systems must meet all applicable requirements, including NIST, FISMA, and ISO 27001.

---

## **6. Review Cycle**
This policy must be reviewed **annually** or when major changes occur.
