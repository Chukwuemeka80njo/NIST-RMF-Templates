# PRIVACY & PII PROCESSING POLICY

Version: 1.0  
Approval Date: (insert date)  
Owner: Information Security Officer (ISO) & Privacy Officer  
Classification: Internal / Restricted  

---

## **1. Purpose**
The purpose of this Privacy & PII Processing Policy is to establish requirements for the collection, processing, storage, transmission, and disposal of Personally Identifiable Information (PII) in accordance with legal, regulatory, and organizational obligations.

---

## **2. Scope**
This policy applies to:

- All employees, contractors, and third parties handling PII  
- All information systems that store, transmit, or process PII  
- All organizational processes that involve PII throughout its lifecycle  

---

## **3. Policy Statements**

### **3.1 PII Identification & Classification**
- All PII must be identified and classified according to sensitivity and regulatory requirements.  
- Systems and processes must document what PII is collected and why.

### **3.2 Transparency & Notice**
- Individuals must be informed of the purpose for collecting their PII through approved privacy notices.  
- Changes to PII usage must be communicated before implementation.

### **3.3 Consent & Authorization**
- Consent must be obtained where legally required.  
- PII must only be processed for approved business purposes.

### **3.4 Data Minimization**
- Only the minimum necessary PII required to accomplish business functions may be collected or retained.

### **3.5 Access Controls**
- Access to PII must be limited to authorized personnel based on role and need-to-know.  
- Privileged access must be approved and documented.

### **3.6 Use & Processing Restrictions**
- PII must not be used for purposes other than those explicitly approved.  
- Secondary uses require a privacy impact review.

### **3.7 Transmission & Storage Protection**
- PII must be encrypted during transmission and at rest.  
- PII must be safeguarded against unauthorized access or modification.

### **3.8 PII Retention & Disposal**
- PII retention periods must be defined and enforced.  
- PII must be securely disposed of at the end of its lifecycle, per organizational procedures.

### **3.9 Accuracy & Correction**
- Individuals must be given the ability to request corrections to inaccurate PII.  
- Systems must support timely updates to stored PII.

### **3.10 Incident Response & Breach Notification**
- All suspected or confirmed PII breaches must be reported immediately to the ISO.  
- Breach notifications must follow legal and regulatory timelines.

### **3.11 Third-Party & Vendor Requirements**
- Vendors processing PII must sign data protection agreements.  
- Third parties must comply with organizational privacy requirements.

### **3.12 Monitoring & Auditing**
- PII handling processes must be routinely audited for compliance.  
- Unauthorized access to PII must trigger alerts and investigations.

### **3.13 Privacy Impact Assessments (PIA)**
- PIAs must be conducted for new systems or major changes involving PII.  
- High-risk processing must undergo additional ISO and legal review.

---

## **4. Roles & Responsibilities**
### **4.1 Privacy Officer**
- Oversees implementation of privacy controls.  
- Ensures regulatory alignment.  

### **4.2 Information Security Officer**
- Ensures security controls protect PII.  
- Coordinates technical safeguards and incident response.

### **4.3 System Owners & Administrators**
- Implement and document privacy controls in their environments.  

### **4.4 Workforce Members**
- Must follow all privacy policies and report suspected breaches.

---

## **5. Exceptions**
Exceptions to this policy require written approval from the Privacy Officer and ISO.

---

## **6. Enforcement**
Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination, as well as legal penalties where applicable.

---

## **7. Review Cycle**
This policy must be reviewed **annually** or upon major changes to legal, regulatory, or system requirements.
