## **1. Purpose**

The purpose of this System & Services Acquisition (SA) Policy is to establish requirements for acquiring, developing, and maintaining information systems, software, and services in a secure, cost-effective, and compliant manner. This policy ensures that security is considered throughout the system development life cycle (SDLC) and that third-party services meet organizational security requirements.

---

## **2. Scope**

This policy applies to:

* All systems and applications developed, acquired, or implemented by the organization
* All third-party vendors, service providers, contractors, and consultants
* All employees involved in procurement, development, integration, or maintenance of technology solutions
* All organizational information systems (on-premises and cloud-hosted)

---

## **3. Policy Statements**

### **3.1 Security Requirements for Acquisitions**

* All new systems and services must define security and privacy requirements prior to acquisition.
* All procurement requests must include cybersecurity evaluation criteria.
* Vendors must agree to comply with organizational security policies and applicable regulations.

### **3.2 Supply Chain Risk Considerations**

* All technology-related acquisitions must include a supply chain security review.
* Vendors shall be assessed for risks related to compromise, tampering, or unauthorized access to systems or components.

### **3.3 System Development Life Cycle (SDLC)**

* All system development must follow an approved SDLC framework.
* Security must be addressed during planning, development, testing, deployment, and maintenance.
* Security impact assessments must be conducted for major system changes.

### **3.4 Software Licensing & Compliance**

* Only legally licensed, authorized software may be installed or used.
* Procurement must validate licensing compliance prior to purchase.
* Software asset inventory must be maintained and updated regularly.

### **3.5 Third-Party & Vendor Agreements**

* All vendor contracts must include security expectations, audit rights, and breach notification clauses.
* Vendors must implement security controls consistent with organizational requirements.
* Vendors storing or processing sensitive data must sign a Data Protection Agreement (DPA).

### **3.6 Secure Code & Product Integrity**

* Acquired software must be evaluated for known vulnerabilities prior to implementation.
* Open-source components must be vetted and monitored for security flaws.
* Systems must be verified to prevent counterfeit, compromised, or unauthorized components.

### **3.7 Cloud Services & Hosted Solutions**

* All cloud vendors must be reviewed for security posture, compliance certifications, and data protection capabilities.
* Cloud service implementations must align with the organizationâ€™s cloud security policies and regulatory requirements.

### **3.8 Maintenance & Support Requirements**

* Systems must include appropriate support, updates, and patching arrangements.
* End-of-life software or hardware may not be acquired or used without formal risk acceptance.

### **3.9 Monitoring & Performance Metrics**

* System and service acquisition activities must be reviewed annually.
* Procurement and IT teams must track vendor performance and contract compliance.

---

## **4. Roles & Responsibilities**

### **Information Security Officer (ISO)**

* Reviews security requirements for all major acquisitions
* Ensures vendors meet security and compliance standards

### **Procurement Department**

* Ensures contracts include required security clauses
* Verifies vendor compliance and licensing legitimacy

### **IT Department**

* Conducts technical assessments of new systems
* Ensures secure deployment and integration

### **System Owners**

* Define functional and security requirements
* Maintain oversight during system acquisition and lifecycle changes

---

## **5. Compliance**

Failure to adhere to this policy may result in disciplinary action, revocation of access privileges, or termination of contracts with non-compliant vendors.

---

## **6. Review & Updates**

This policy must be reviewed **annually** or upon significant changes to procurement processes, security requirements, or regulations.
