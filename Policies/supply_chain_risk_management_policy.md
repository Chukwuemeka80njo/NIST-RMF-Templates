# SUPPLY CHAIN RISK MANAGEMENT POLICY

**Version:** 1.0
**Approval Date:** (insert date)
**Owner:** Information Security Officer (ISO) & Procurement Manager
**Classification:** Internal / Restricted

---

## **1. Purpose**

The purpose of this Supply Chain Risk Management (SR) Policy is to establish requirements for identifying, assessing, monitoring, and mitigating risks associated with suppliers, vendors, contractors, service providers, and external partners who support, develop, or deliver information systems, components, or services.

This policy ensures the integrity, security, and resilience of the organization’s supply chain to prevent compromise, tampering, or unauthorized access.

---

## **2. Scope**

This policy applies to:

* All suppliers, vendors, and third-party service providers
* All employees involved in procurement, contracting, or vendor management
* All information systems, components, software, hardware, and services obtained from external parties
* All cloud, SaaS, PaaS, IaaS, and hosted service providers
* All personnel with access to organization systems or sensitive data

---

## **3. Policy Statements**

### **3.1 Vendor Risk Assessments**

* All vendors must undergo a formal risk assessment before onboarding.
* Risk assessments must evaluate security posture, regulatory compliance, financial stability, and operational reliability.
* Vendors with high-risk profiles require ISO approval before engagement.

### **3.2 Supply Chain Threat Awareness**

* All procurement and IT teams must be trained to identify supply chain threats such as counterfeit components, code tampering, and unauthorized third-party dependencies.
* The organization shall leverage threat intelligence to monitor risks related to vendors and supply chain disruptions.

### **3.3 Security Requirements in Contracts**

* All contracts must include security clauses addressing:

  * Data protection and privacy obligations
  * Incident reporting timeframes
  * Right to audit and security assessments
  * Breach liability and remediation requirements
* Vendors must adhere to the organization's cybersecurity and privacy policies.

### **3.4 Verification of Components & Services**

* Hardware and software must be acquired from trusted, reputable sources.
* Systems must be validated to ensure integrity and prevent counterfeit or compromised components.
* All third-party code, libraries, and firmware must be verified prior to deployment.

### **3.5 Continuous Monitoring of Vendor Security**

* Vendor security controls must be reviewed at least annually.
* High-risk vendors must undergo continuous monitoring, including:

  * Vulnerability alerts
  * Compliance status updates
  * Security audit results

### **3.6 Multi-Tier Supplier Oversight**

* Vendors must disclose their own critical suppliers when relevant.
* Subcontractors that impact organizational systems or data must meet equivalent security standards.

### **3.7 Termination & Offboarding Controls**

* Upon contract termination, vendors must return or delete organizational data.
* Access accounts must be disabled immediately.
* Final security checks and confirmations must be documented.

### **3.8 Incident Reporting & Response**

* Vendors must report security incidents affecting the organization within defined timeframes (e.g., 24–72 hours).
* Joint investigation and coordination may be required depending on impact.

### **3.9 Regulatory & Compliance Requirements**

* Vendors must comply with applicable laws and regulations such as:

  * NIST
  * FISMA
  * GDPR (if applicable)
  * HIPAA (if applicable)
* Documentation must be retained to demonstrate compliance.

---

## **4. Roles & Responsibilities**

### **Information Security Officer (ISO)**

* Approves risk assessments for high-risk vendors.
* Ensures vendor compliance with organizational security requirements.

### **Procurement Department**

* Ensures security requirements are embedded in all contracts.
* Manages vendor onboarding and lifecycle documentation.

### **IT Department**

* Performs technical evaluations of components and services.
* Monitors supply chain-related technical risks.

### **Vendor Owners / Business Units**

* Define service expectations and validate vendor performance.
* Monitor vendor relationships and escalate issues.

---

## **5. Compliance**

Failure to comply with this policy may result in contract termination, restricted vendor usage, disciplinary action, or legal consequences.

---

## **6. Review & Updates**

This policy must be reviewed **annually** or upon major changes to vendor environment, supply chain threats, or regulatory requirements.
