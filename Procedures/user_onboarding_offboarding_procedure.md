# USER ONBOARDING & OFFBOARDING PROCEDURE  
Version: 1.0  
Approval Date: (insert date)  
Owner: Information Security Officer (ISO) & Human Resources (HR)  
Classification: Internal / Restricted  

---

## **1. Purpose**  
The purpose of this procedure is to define standardized steps for securely onboarding new personnel and offboarding departing personnel to ensure proper access control, accountability, and protection of organizational information systems.

---

## **2. Scope**  
This procedure applies to:  
- All employees, contractors, vendors, and interns  
- All information systems requiring authentication or authorization  
- HR, IT, Security, and hiring managers involved in the process  

---

## **3. Roles & Responsibilities**  
### **Human Resources (HR)**  
- Initiates onboarding and offboarding events  
- Ensures employment verification and background checks  
- Communicates personnel changes to IT and Security

### **Hiring Manager**  
- Defines access needs  
- Submits onboarding access requests  
- Ensures equipment and training requirements are met

### **IT Support**  
- Provisions and deprovisions accounts  
- Issues equipment and configures system access  
- Collects assets during offboarding

### **Information Security Officer (ISO)**  
- Approves privileged access  
- Ensures security requirements are met  
- Monitors for unauthorized access after offboarding

---

## **4. Onboarding Procedure**

### **4.1 Pre-Employment Setup (HR)**  
1. HR verifies identity and employment eligibility.  
2. HR creates an onboarding ticket or form including:  
   - Employee name  
   - Job title & department  
   - Start date  
   - Access requirements  
   - Equipment needs  

---

### **4.2 Access Requirements (Manager)**  
1. Manager determines required systems and permissions.  
2. Manager submits a **User Access Request** for all systems.  
3. Privileged access requires ISO approval.  

---

### **4.3 IT Account Provisioning**  
IT must:  
1. Create network, email, and application accounts.  
2. Apply role-based permissions (least privilege).  
3. Enable MFA for all required systems.  
4. Update inventory with assigned equipment.  

---

---

## **5. Offboarding Procedure**

### **5.1 Offboarding Initiation (HR)**  
1. HR notifies IT and Security **at least 24 hours prior** to termination.  
2. For involuntary or immediate terminations:  
   - HR must notify IT/Security **prior to the meeting**  
   - IT must be prepared to disable access immediately  

---

### **5.2 Access Revocation (IT)**  
1. IT disables all user accounts immediately upon HR notification.  
2. Remove access from:  
   - Network/domain  
   - Email and cloud services  
   - Business applications  
   - Privileged/administrative systems  
3. Remove user from group memberships and distribution lists.  
4. Document all actions in the offboarding ticket.

---

### **5.3 Asset Recovery**  
Manager and IT must retrieve:  
- Laptops  
- Badges and access cards  
- Mobile devices  
- Tokens or MFA devices  
- Hard drives or removable media  

All returned equipment must be:  
- Logged  
- Inspected  
- Sanitized  
- Reimaged if required  

---

### **5.4 Knowledge Transfer**  
Manager ensures completion of:  
- Work handover  
- Status reports  
- Transfer of project ownership  
- Removal from meetings and calendars  

---

### **5.5 Security Validation (ISO)**  
The ISO must verify:  
- All accounts are disabled  
- No unauthorized activity occurred during offboarding  
- All privileged accounts were revoked  
- All access logs show proper termination activity  

---

## **6. Documentation & Record Retention**  
All onboarding and offboarding records must be retained for **1 year**, including:  
- Access request forms  
- AUP and training acknowledgments  
- Equipment inventory  
- Termination approvals  
- Audit logs of account changes  

---

## **7. Audit Requirements**  
- Quarterly user access reviews  
- Monthly privileged access review  
- Annual assessment of onboarding/offboarding process
